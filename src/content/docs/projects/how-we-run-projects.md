Arillus uses a **matrix model**: functions are your permanent home; project teams are temporary, assembled per project and dissolved after.

## Project Team Template

Every role is either **dedicated** (this project is their main assignment) or **fractional** (spans several projects).

### Dedicated core

| Role | Function / Level | One-line duty |
|---|---|---|
| Project Coordinator | Delivery, L1–L3 | Runs standups, tracks progress, day-to-day point of contact for status and blockers |
| Product Analyst / BA | Product, L1–L2 | Writes user stories, maintains the backlog, documents requirements and decisions |
| Tech Lead | Engineering, L3+ | Owns day-to-day technical decisions, reviews code, unblocks developers while coding the harder parts |
| Developers | Engineering, L1–L3 | Build, test and deliver features per stories and technical direction |
| QA | L1–L2 | Executes test plans, verifies releases, reports defects |
| Designer | (design phase) | Produces the full set of screens and assets, then rolls off |

### Fractional senior ring

| Role | Level | One-line duty |
|---|---|---|
| Engagement Owner | Partner / L5+, or L3 for small projects | Owns the client relationship, scope, commercials and final escalations |
| Product Owner | L3+ | Owns discovery, scope decisions and requirement sign-off with the client |
| Architect / Tech Reviewer | L4–L5 | Approves technical approach at kickoff; reviews at milestones |
| Senior QA | L3+ | Reviews test plans upfront; spot-checks coverage and quality |
| Senior Designer | L3+ | Sets UX direction early; reviews design output at checkpoints |
| DevOps | shared | Infrastructure, CI/CD and deployments across projects |

## Staffing Rules

1. **Role definitions are fixed; the minimum level to hold a role scales with project size and risk.** Small friendly project → L2 coordinator is fine. Large high-stakes engagement → L3+ coordinator, L4+ Engagement Owner.
2. **Fractional roles are skipped when the dedicated person is already senior enough.** An L5 Tech Lead needs no separate architect. Exception: the Engagement Owner always stays separate from the delivery team — someone slightly outside must own the commercial relationship.
3. **Dedicated ≠ one project.** A coordinator can span 2–4 small projects; a tech lead can stretch to 2 small ones. "Dedicated" means primary assignment, not exclusivity.
4. **Juniors grow as deputies.** On large projects, an L1 shadows the L3 in the same role — runs the standup while the senior observes, drafts reports the senior reviews.
5. **Continuous-context roles are dedicated; checkpoint roles are fractional.** If the work is interrupt-driven and daily (tech lead), dedicate it. If it's review-driven and periodic (architect), fractionalize it.

## Reporting on a Project (day-to-day)

- Developers and QA → **Tech Lead** for technical work
- Everyone → **Project Coordinator** for status, blockers, timelines
- Coordinator → **Engagement Owner** for escalations, scope and client issues
- Product Analyst → **Product Owner** for requirement decisions

The project line directs work. It does **not** do appraisals, and it dissolves when the project ends.

---

## Reporting in Your Function (career)

Your real manager is your **function head**. They own your appraisal, growth and promotion — informed by written feedback from every project you touched.

**The bridge:** at project end (or quarterly on long projects), the Engagement Owner and project leads submit short written feedback on each team member. Function heads aggregate this across projects, judge it against level definitions, and calibrate ratings together before finalizing.

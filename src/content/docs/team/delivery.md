Delivery is responsible for getting work out the door — on time, within scope, and with the client relationship intact. Delivery people own the day-to-day coordination of projects: they run standups, track progress, surface blockers, manage the client's expectations, and make sure nothing falls through the gap between functions. They are the connective tissue of every project team.

The function is led by **Anandu** (Partner, Product & Design, Delivery).

---

## Delivery Ladder

Each level assumes everything in the levels below it.

### L1 — Associate Project Coordinator

*Scope: coordination tasks within a project*

- Runs standups under a senior coordinator's supervision; follows an established format
- Updates trackers, boards and status documents accurately and on time
- Takes meeting notes and circulates action items promptly
- Chases status from team members when updates are overdue — proactively, not reactively
- Escalates blockers to the senior coordinator immediately; does not sit on problems

### L2 — Project Coordinator

*Scope: a small project end-to-end*

- Independently coordinates a small project from kickoff to close: standups, status tracking, release coordination, client updates
- Owns the day-to-day status and blockers for the full team — developers, QA, designer, analyst
- Communicates routine progress updates to the client clearly and on time, without being prompted
- Keeps the engagement owner informed of anything unusual; knows what to escalate and when
- Starts identifying process gaps on their projects and proposing improvements

### L3 — Senior Coordinator / Project Manager

*Scope: a larger or more complex project; Engagement Owner for small projects*

- Manages larger, more complex, or higher-stakes projects with multiple workstreams
- Handles client communication and escalations directly — delivers difficult news clearly, manages expectations professionally
- Can act as **Engagement Owner** on small projects: owns the client relationship, scope decisions, and commercial communications
- Mentors L1–L2 coordinators on the same project; helps them grow without taking work from them
- Anticipates delivery risks before they become problems: scope drift, team capacity, client expectation gaps
- Prepares and presents project status reports to clients independently

### L4 — Delivery Manager

*Scope: multiple engagements simultaneously*

- Acts as **Engagement Owner** across several concurrent engagements: owns scope, commercials, client relationships and final escalations
- Manages scope change requests end-to-end: impact assessment, commercial negotiation, documentation and sign-off
- First management layer for coordinators: owns their appraisals, growth conversations and day-to-day support
- Identifies and fixes systemic delivery problems across the portfolio — not just project-by-project

### L5 — Senior Delivery Manager

*Scope: the firm's project portfolio*

- Owns a portfolio of client accounts at the relationship level: client health, renewal risk, growth opportunities
- Sets delivery standards and processes for the firm; ensures consistency of client experience
- Develops the L3–L4 layer: mentors Delivery Managers, helps them grow into multi-account ownership
- Measured by client satisfaction, delivery margin, and the capability of the coordinators around them

### L6 — Head of Delivery

*Scope: the firm*

- Owns the delivery practice itself: processes, standards, margins, and the firm's reputation for reliable delivery
- Trusted by clients as the most senior delivery voice in the firm
- Develops and stretches the L4–L5 layer
- Measured on the long-term health, margin and consistency of delivery across all Arillus engagements

## Levels

Each function has a ladder from L1 to L7. Levels are defined by scope, autonomy and impact — not tenure. The full L1–L7 skeleton exists on paper from day one; senior levels open up as the firm grows.

## Engineering & QA Ladder

Each level assumes everything below it.

### L1 — Associate Software Engineer
Delivers well-defined tasks with guidance; writes working code that passes review, though reviews catch significant issues. Learning the codebase, tooling and team practices. Asks for help early rather than staying stuck. *Scope: a task (days).*

### L2 — Software Engineer
Independently delivers features end-to-end from a written story — including tests and edge cases — with reviews catching only minor issues. Debugs own problems before escalating. Estimates own work reasonably. Starting to review L1 code. *Scope: a feature (1–2 weeks).*

### L3 — Senior Software Engineer
Owns a module or workstream; designs solutions, not just implements them. Anticipates edge cases, performance and security. Reviews others' code as a norm, mentors L1–L2s, and can be **Tech Lead on a standard project** — breaking down work, unblocking teammates, communicating technical status to the coordinator and client. *Scope: a project's technical delivery (months).*

### L4 — Lead Software Engineer
Tech-leads larger or complex projects; makes architecture decisions within them. Acts as **fractional Architect/Reviewer** for projects led by L3s. Handles technical client conversations independently — pushback, trade-offs, feasibility. Contributes to pre-sales estimation. Raises the team's bar through standards, not just personal output. *Scope: multiple workstreams / the hardest single projects.*

### L5 — Senior Lead Software Engineer
Technical oversight across a portfolio of projects; escalation point for the firm's hardest technical problems. Owns cross-project concerns: technology choices, reusable components, engineering standards. Routine pre-sales solutioning. Likely the first Engineering Manager — appraising and developing L1–L3s. *Scope: several projects / a sub-practice.*

### L6 — Principal Engineer
Owns the engineering practice itself: how Arillus builds software — stack decisions, quality standards, hiring bar, technical reputation. Trusted by clients as the firm's senior technical voice. Develops the L4–L5 layer. Measured on the capability of the whole engineering org, not personal delivery. *Scope: the firm.*

> **Calibration notes:** The L2→L3 promotion is the most consequential — it means "can be trusted with a project's technical delivery." Client-facing ability appears at L3 and is mandatory by L4: an engineer who cannot be put in front of a client caps at L3 regardless of technical skill.

## Product & Design Ladder

### Product track

| Level | Title | Summary |
|---|---|---|
| L1 | Associate Product Analyst | Gathers requirements, writes user stories under supervision |
| L2 | Product Analyst / BA | Owns requirements for a module or small project independently |
| L3 | Senior Analyst / Associate PM | Owns discovery and scope for a full project; runs client workshops |
| L4 | Product Manager | Owns product direction across a large engagement; manages client stakeholders directly |
| L5 | Senior PM / Product Consultant | Spans multiple engagements; shapes solutions in pre-sales; mentors |
| L6 | Principal / Head of Product | Defines the firm's product practice — methods, tooling, hiring bar |

### Design track

Associate Designer → Designer → Senior Designer → Lead Designer → Design Manager / Principal Designer, mirroring the same level logic.

## Delivery Ladder

| Level | Title | Summary |
|---|---|---|
| L1 | Associate Project Coordinator | Runs standups, updates trackers, chases status under supervision |
| L2 | Project Coordinator | Independently coordinates small projects end-to-end |
| L3 | Senior Coordinator / Project Manager | Manages larger projects; handles client communication and escalations; Engagement Owner for small projects |
| L4 | Delivery Manager | Engagement Owner for multiple engagements; owns scope and commercials; first management layer for coordinators |
| L5 | Senior Delivery Manager | Owns a portfolio of accounts; pre-sales and estimation |
| L6 | Head of Delivery | Owns the delivery practice — margins, processes, client satisfaction firm-wide |
